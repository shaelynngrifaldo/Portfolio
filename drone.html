Orthomosaic

This project demonstrates the drone mapping process using Pix4D software. Images were captured via sUAS flight missions, processed to identify ground control points, and transformed into a georeferenced orthomosaic. The workflow includes initial image overlay alignment, keypoint matching for accuracy, and final orthorectification.

Project Findings & Lessons Learned

Our project focused on comparing drone-derived GPS measurements with traditional ground surveys. We specifically evaluated accuracy for length, width, and positional data and identified challenges to improve future UAV-based geospatial surveys.

The key findings showed no statistically significant differences in length, width, or positional accuracy between drone and ground measurements. Elevation data was less reliable due to lack of GCPs and environmental challenges. The drone saved considerable time, making it useful for larger or hard-to-reach areas.
